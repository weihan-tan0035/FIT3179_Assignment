Influenza Cases in New York

New York is a Northeastern state of the United States of America(USA or simply just US). Being one of the original thirteen colonies that formed the United States, it is the forth most populous state in the US, and is also home to New York City, the most populous city in the US. Often described as the cultural, financial and media capital of the world, the state borders New Jersey, Pennsylvania, Connecticut, Massachusetts, Vermont, and Canadian Provinces of Quebec and Ontario.

Counties and Regions in new york state

There are in total 62 counties covering 5 regions, which are New York City, Capital District, Central New York, New York Metropolitan Area, and Western New York..

This dot graph shows that,when population increases, number of infections increases.

Cases Between Seasons

We can see from here that the influenza season of 2011-2012 was the lowest. It was also at the time the lowest and shortest recorded peak for influenza like illnesses (ILI). This was due to the season begining late and was mild compared to most previous seasons. ILIs only peaked during mid-March, and ILIs reached the baseline for only 1 week during the season.

Ratio of Infected and Healthy population

In general, none of the seasons in the dataset ever reach more than 25% total population infected except one outlier which was the 2017-2018 season. The 2017-2018 season had roughly 40% of the state's population being infected by the influenza.
